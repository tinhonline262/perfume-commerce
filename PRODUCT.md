# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Essence serves three primary user contexts: fragrance shoppers comparing and buying perfumes, registered customers managing cart, checkout, profile, and order history, and admins maintaining products, users, and orders. Shoppers need confidence while browsing unfamiliar scents, enough product detail to choose well, and a checkout path that feels dependable rather than ornamental. Admins need efficient, predictable controls for catalog and order operations without losing the storefront's refined tone.

## Product Purpose

The product is a full-stack perfume ecommerce application for discovering, filtering, purchasing, and managing fragrance products. Success means customers can move from discovery to purchase with low friction, understand what they are buying, return to their order/account state reliably, and admins can keep the catalog and fulfillment data accurate.

## Brand Personality

Quiet luxury, calm, and trustworthy. The interface should feel like a restrained fragrance shop: editorial enough to support sensory product discovery, but disciplined enough that shopping, checkout, account, and admin workflows remain clear and fast.

## Anti-references

Avoid flashy beauty-campaign effects, generic SaaS/admin UI, cluttered marketplace density, loud discount-driven retail patterns, and decorative motion that competes with shopping tasks. Admin and account areas should not look detached from the storefront, but they should also avoid display-heavy styling where users need labels, forms, tables, and decisions.

## Design Principles

1. Keep shopping confidence ahead of ornament: every visual decision should make scent discovery, price, availability, cart state, and checkout intent easier to understand.
2. Let quiet luxury show through restraint: use calm hierarchy, considered copy, and product imagery without turning task screens into campaigns.
3. Preserve one product vocabulary across storefront, account, and admin: buttons, forms, tables, loading, empty, and error states should feel consistent across roles.
4. Make admin work efficient by default: high-frequency management actions should be direct, predictable, and recoverable.
5. Treat reliability as part of the brand: authentication, cart, checkout, order history, and management flows should communicate state clearly and never leave users guessing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Prioritize readable contrast, keyboard-operable navigation and forms, visible focus states, non-color-only status indicators, color-blind-safe semantic states, useful labels and errors, resilient responsive layouts, and reduced-motion alternatives for transitions or reveals.
